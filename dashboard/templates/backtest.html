Is the gap to the market closing?

Trading-mechanics work is paused as of 2026-07-20 — three independent tests (live prices, full backtest, same-day proof) agreed the model has no edge over Kalshi's own prices, and no amount of sizing or exit logic can manufacture one. This panel is the standing answer to “has anything changed?”, so that question gets settled from accumulated evidence rather than a fresh manual audit each time.

Skill is the number that matters, not Brier. The model was already reasonably calibrated against outcomes while losing badly to the market, so Brier alone could improve for months without anything becoming tradeable. Positive skill = beat the market on held-out rows; 0 = merely reproduced the price; negative = trading it is worse than not.

Probability reliability (held-out backtest)

Does a "70% chance" bracket actually win about 70% of the time? This fits the model on the older 70% of settled days and checks it on the held-out most-recent 30% it never saw, then pools every city and metric together (any single thin market has too few days to judge a bucket on its own). This is the calibration criterion the paper-trading validation bar refers to. Re-run with uv run scripts/run_backtest.py.
