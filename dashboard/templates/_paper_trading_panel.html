View full rationale Hide full rationale
This strategy has no edge. Do not put real capital behind it. Measured against live settlements on 462 distinct settled markets (77 events, 20 cities): the model’s Brier score is 0.1224 against the market’s 0.0048 — the market is ~25× more accurate, and beats the model in 19 of 20 cities. Across the 171 markets where the model claimed more than 10 points of edge, 0 resolved in its favour. The “edge” was measuring the model’s own staleness, not mispricing: bracket probabilities ignored the temperature already recorded that day, while the market could see it.

The criteria below were never reached — they are a sample-size bar, and this is a structural failure that more samples cannot fix. Tightening the edge threshold makes it worse, because a larger claimed edge selects for a larger model error. Per the pre-committed rule below: back to the forecasting core, not more automation and not real money. The root cause is fixed in weather/probability.py (observation_conditioned_bracket_probability, live as normal-v4-observation-conditioned); this bar stays FAILED until scripts/run_backtest.py reports TRADEABLE — i.e. the model actually beats the market out-of-sample — and a fresh batch of paper trades then clears the sample-size criteria below on top of that.

Set 2026-07-19, before knowing how this goes — the whole point of writing it down now is to judge results against a bar fixed in advance, not against whichever number looks good this week. Prompted by an external review of this whole approach; full context in this project's memory. Progress numbers below are live; the thresholds themselves are a reasonable starting judgment call, not a rigorous power analysis — revisit them once there's real data to revisit them with.

  • Sample size: {{ closed_since_reset|length }} of a 100-trade minimum before drawing any conclusion; 300+ settled trades and 60+ calendar days (currently {{ days_since_reset if days_since_reset is not none else 0 }}) before a real go/no-go call — both conditions, not either. A burst of correlated trades on one bad night isn't "enough data," as the very first batch already showed.
  • Calibration: once a predicted-probability bucket has ~20+ settled trades, its real hit rate should land within roughly 10-15 percentage points of what was predicted. A persistent miss in a well-populated bucket — not noise in a small one — means pause and re-fit, not push forward hoping it resolves itself.
  • EV tracking: Expected P&L and Realized P&L (above) should move together over time. A single bad correlated night isn't the signal to react to; a sustained, widening gap over multiple weeks is.
  • Not a pass/fail metric on its own: win rate — see its tooltip above for why.

If the bar is met: small real capital, with circuit breakers (daily/consecutive-loss caps — not yet built) live from day one, not before. If it's missed: back to the forecasting core, not more automation and not real money "to see what happens."
