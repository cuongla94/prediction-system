How validated is this? — read before trading ▸

Improved, partially validated — and how much varies a lot by city and metric right now. The original 6 cities' High Temperature markets (NYC, Chicago, Philadelphia, Austin, Denver, Miami) have the deepest history (420–460+ settled days) and are the only ones cross-checked against NOAA's independent station data so far (2026-07-18: Kalshi's own settlement results agreed on 3,759 of 3,762 comparable days, 99.9%).

Where there's enough history to check it, the day-ahead forecast's bias isn't constant through the year — NYC's flips from +2.7°F cold in January to -1.6°F warm in July — so those markets use a month-specific correction instead of one flat number.

The 14 cities added 2026-07-19, and every city's Low Temperature market (including the original 6's), have much less history (roughly 75–150 settled days) and haven't been through that NOAA cross-check yet — treat those specifically as less proven, not equally validated just because they're on the same dashboard. Exact per-city numbers are on the Backtest results page. A fatter-tailed distribution was tried earlier and didn't help, so this stays a standard bell curve everywhere. Predictions above ~40% are still thinly sampled across the board, too — treat those as exploratory, not a trading signal.
